AC-DC Difference

AC-DC Difference is a measurement used to determine the thermal equivalence between DC and AC values. This is most accurately done using a thermal transfer standard (TTS) which can take on many forms.

General Concepts

There are two main uses for ac-dc difference devices: measurement of other types of devices and measurement of another ac-dc device. Measurements of other types of devices can be anything from a source to a meter. I will not delve into this topic at this time.

Measurements of another ac-dc device is the most common calibration configuration. For this measurement, two ac-dc devices are connected using a tee and measure the same electrical signal (constant voltage or current). The signal is applied through a timed sequence switching through both dc polarities and ac values (DC+, AC, DC-,...). During the measurement sequence the average of the dc polarities is matched with the ac signal based on one of the devices. The difference in the output of the second device with the correction of the first considered is the ac-dc difference.

Requirements

  • All instruments in known good condition.
  • Stable normal measurement environment.
  • Note: The sources and meters do not need to be calibrated, but they need to be in good working condition. A calibration satisfis this condition, but a simple verification and characterization is sufficient.

Traceability

Tracebility of ac-dc standards for most labs goes to the calibration certificate that shows the link to a national lab.

Tracebility of ac-dc standards at national labs is complex. There are a number of well characterized specialized standards that the national lab uses to estabilish ac-dc difference. These special standards might for example only be for frequencies below 400 Hz or for frequencies over 10 kHz, low voltages, low currents, etc. The specialized standards are used to create an ensemble ac-dc value which is then assigned to working standards.
